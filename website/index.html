Our Story

It is with much excitement that we share with you that after time in discernment, we are planning to spend a year in Mbeya, Tanzania serving as missionaries in the Konde Diocese.

Our home church, Zion Lutheran East Petersburg, is in Christian partnership (along with our Synod) with Forest Lutheran Church in Tanzania. For the past decade our congregations’ partnership has come in the form of prayer, giving and receiving of gifts, and financial support. For a number of years we have discussed the depth and Christian nature of this relationship. We both strongly feel that to be in true partnership, we are to be present in our relationship.

Over the past year and half, we have been discerning what we feel is a call to serve in the Konde Diocese in Tanzania. We have been to several meetings with our Synod’s Konde committee to gather information to determine if this is the right choice for us and our family. After many meetings, conversations, and prayer together, we have decided that serving in Tanzania is a true call and we are committed to following that call.

We have been formally invited to serve in the Konde Diocese beginning in the summer of 2016 for one full year. Our invitation has been extended from the Bishop Israel-Peter and the Provost from the Science & Technology University in Mbeya (where Forest Lutheran is located). We will both be teaching classes, and in return, have been offered free faculty housing on campus. Angie will be teaching English and volunteering time with Forest Lutheran serving in ministry with families and youth. Andrew will be teaching computer science courses at the university and working with professors to help them advance their programs to better meet the global IT market. Andrew will also be working with hospitals and medical professionals and serving as a conduit to the medical community in Lancaster County and Pennsylvania.

Our time in missionary service will also include developing relationships with Lancaster County schools and schools in Mbeya. We have already begun taking steps with our children’s school, East Petersburg Elementary, to establish a relationship with schools in Tanzania. The children from both schools will be able to share real-time and recorded experiences. This benefits the kids of both the Tanzanian and American schools. Educational, cultural, and life experiences can be easily shared and learned from one another through the advances of technology.

Beyond the responsibilities of teaching, we will be very active in service and in relationships with the Lutheran church. We will be learning how we can better strengthen our relationship and support among the churches, learning how our Synod can help support free trade, and overall how we can all learn and benefit from our faith based relationship.

Our children; Henry 11, Lucy 8, and Wyatt 5, are excited about this adventure – what kid wouldn’t be excited about visiting Africa!? However, we feel as though they should have some responsibility as well. Their interactions with other children will no doubt leave an impression on those they meet. Our children, just by virtue of play and conversation will be installing first world ideas and thoughts into the minds of their third world peers. Those ideas can grow into realties that we cannot imagine today. We are excited about the impact our children will have on the lives of the children in Mbeya, and we’re excited about what this experience will do for our children spiritually.

In preparation for our trip, we are diligently working to conserve funds for travel and our living expenses by building our savings, and reducing debt and expenses, including selling our vehicles. Even with these measures, we need to raise $30,000 to support our family’s mission work for the year in Tanzania. We are selling t-shirts and planning community fundraisers, like the Isaac’s dinner you may have attended, and we are asking for generous support from grant funders, businesses, congregations, family, friends, and caring people like you.

We will be keeping you all posted on our planning efforts throughout the year and while we are overseas, we will be maintaining a website that we will document our experiences and our work. You can follow our journey at www.hammermission.org.

Thank you for your prayers and generous support of our mission work.

Peace,
Andrew & Angie Hammer
Henry, Lucy, & Wyatt
